Response Analysis Instructions (Click to expand)
We are creating an Artificially Intelligent(AI) system that can generate Conversational-Responses for Questions. We expect the Response to be a complete, grammatical sentence which correctly answers the Question.
For each instance we show you a "Question" and its correct "Answer" along with the generated "Response" from one of the AI systems. Your goal is to evaluate the generated Response's quality and select the most suitable option. We describe the options with some of the examples in the Guidance column of the table below:
Options Guidance
correct answer ✘; complete-sentence ✘ Mark this option if, the Response doesn't answer the Question and is a sentence fragment instead of the whole(cannot be considered a complete sentence when looked at independently or in context of the question). A sentence which is not complete is by-default considered to be grammatically incorrect.
Example:
Question:"what does the mother wasp provide for her eggs when they hatch ?"
Answer:"caterpillars"

Response:"centipede for eggs"
OR
Response:"egg shells"
correct answer ✔; complete-sentence ✘ The Response answers the Question correctly but it is a sentence fragment instead of the whole(cannot be considered a complete sentence when looked at independently or in context of the question).
Example:
Question:"what does the mother wasp provide for her eggs when they hatch ?"
Answer:"caterpillars"

Response:"caterpillars for eggs"
OR
Response:"caterpillars"
correct answer ✘; complete-sentence ✔; grammaticality ? The Response doesn't directly or indirectly answer the Question but is a complete-sentence. This Response seems like it is on the right topic but doesn't quite answer the Question. It may or may not be grammatically correct.
Example:
Question:"what does the mother wasp provide for her eggs when they hatch ?"
Answer:"caterpillars"

Response:"Some insects display a rudimentary sense of numbers, such as the solitary wasps that prey upon a single species."
correct answer ✔; complete-sentence ✔; grammaticality ✘ The Response might contain the answer and is also a complete-sentence but isn't a viable response because of too many grammatical or semantic mistakes.
Example:
Question:"what does the mother wasp provide for her eggs when they hatch ?"
Answer:"caterpillars"

Response:"it provides to caterpillars"
OR
Response:"caterpillars , it provides as"
correct answer ✔; complete-sentence ✔; grammaticality ✔ The Response answers the question correctly and is fluent, natural and human-like.
Example:
Question:"what does the mother wasp provide for her eggs when they hatch ?"
Answer:"caterpillars"

Response:"she provides caterpillars for her eggs"
OR
Response:"provides caterpillars for her eggs"
